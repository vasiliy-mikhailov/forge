Predicate: P10 + P14. Path:
[`phase-preliminary/adr/0022-measurable-motivation-chain-okrs.md`](../phase-preliminary/adr/0022-measurable-motivation-chain-okrs.md).

**Symptom.** Pre-this-commit section heading was `## Motivation chain`
(60 files) or bare `## Motivation` (17 ADRs). Post-ADR-0019 + ADR-0021

### F3. Double-substitution bug caught in same commit

Predicate: meta. Bug post-mortem worth recording (per
[`postmortems.md`](../phase-g-implementation-governance/postmortems.md)
intake pattern).

**Symptom.** First sweep ran the heading regex AND the prose regex
in one pass; the prose regex matched "Motivation chain" inside the
NEW heading `## Measurable motivation chain (OKRs)` and produced
`## Measurable measurable motivation chain (OKRs) (OKRs)` in 85
files. Caught immediately by `motivation-measurability-report.py`
returning 48 walks instead of 120 — the new regex didn't match the
double-substituted heading.

**Note.** Cleanup sweep replaced
`Measurable measurable motivation chain (OKRs) (OKRs)` →
`Measurable motivation chain (OKRs)` everywhere. Lesson for
postmortems.md intake (queued, not added in this audit since
pre-prod catch counts in audit, not in postmortems): when sweeping
a heading rename, run the heading sweep + prose sweep in TWO
passes with different anchors, not one pass. Or: do the heading
swap with a placeholder token first, then do the prose swap, then
swap the placeholder back. Architect-velocity loss: ~3 minutes
(caught immediately by the same-commit verification step).
**This pre-prod catch counts toward the next audit's
pre_prod_share** (the verification harness IS the gate that caught
it).
